Our History

Leroy's Ice Cream began as a small shop in the heart of Sun Valley. Our founder, Leroy Thompson, had a vision to create delicious ice cream while giving back to the community. Since opening our doors in 2005, we've grown to become a beloved local staple, serving customers from all over the world.

From sourcing fresh ingredients to supporting local charities, Leroy's has always focused on community and quality. We're proud to donate 100% of our profits to local schools, parks, and non-profit organizations.

The History of Ketchum Idaho:

Mining Boom:

The discovery of silver near the Big Wood River in 1860 led to the settlement of the Wood River Valley. The town was originally named Leadville, but was renamed after David Ketchum, a trapper and guide who staked a claim in the area. Ketchum became the supply base for the upper Wood River mines and a smelting center for the Warm Springs mining district.

Sheep Ranching:

After the silver market crashed in 1890, the town transitioned to sheep ranching. By 1918, the sheep population in the valley had grown to 2.65 million, making Ketchum one of the largest sheep-shipping centers in the West.

Sun Valley Resort

In 1936, the Union Pacific Railroad commissioned Count Felix Schaffgotsch to find a site for a ski resort in the Rocky Mountains. Schaffgotsch chose Ketchum, and a four-story lodge and ski runs were built in just seven months. The resort became popular with celebrities like Gary Cooper and Ernest Hemingway making it even more famous.
